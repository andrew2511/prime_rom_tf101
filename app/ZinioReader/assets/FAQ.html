About the Library

What does the download icon next to an issue mean?

To read a magazine, you must first download the files to your Android device. The download icon is shown next to issues that haven't yet been downloaded. When you select one, you can preview the magazine as the pages load.

Why is a Wi-Fi connection recommended to download an issue?

Magazine files can be quite large, which means they could take a very long time to download over a cellular network. To make sure you download the files quickly and without errors, you should be connected to the Internet via a Wi-Fi network.

I want to read my magazines on a plane, subway or ferry.

To read a magazine, you must first download the files to your Android device. Make sure to download your magazines while connected to a Wi-Fi network for best performance. Once an issue is downloaded, you can read it anywhere - no network connection necessary.

Why is my Android library different than my Zinio web library?

If you have an existing Zinio library you may notice that not all of your publications show up in the Zinio Android application. This is because we are still in the process of translating the thousands of titles in our library to the Android. While we have many titles on the Android device, mostly are the latest issues that are currently available. Zinio is working diligently to make all titles and past issues viewable as well.

How do I delete a magazine issue?

You can delete magazine files from your Android device to free up memory. You will still be entitled to the issue, and it will be marked as available for download in your library. From the Library list screen, tap the Android device MENU button. Then select "delete" from the menu. Select the magazines you'd like deleted, then tap the "delete" button at the bottom of the screen.

Can I change the list view in my library?

Using the "Date|Title" toggle at the top of the page, you can reorder the list of your publications so they are grouped by month published or by the title of the magazine.

How can I read magazines not in my library?

Tap the "Shop" button at the bottom of the Library screen to see a full list of titles available for Android. From here, you can purchase a single issue or an annual subscription using your credit card.

Why is there a "New" icon next to some issues?

These are unread, recently published issues.

How do I renew a subscription?

We will send you an email when your subscription is about to expire. Click on that link and enter your payment details.

Reading a Publication

Paging forward

Once you have selected a magazine, you can page forward or backwards by using the touch screen "swipe" feature. Swipe left or right to navigate.

Zooming

You can zoom into a page a number of ways. Double tap a region to zoom quickly to 2x. Reverse pinch for fine-tuned zooming in. Pinch to zoom out and drag to pan around the page. Zooming on the high quality images doesn't work until high quality images are loaded. It's recommended to wait until the progress bar for magazine download to complete for a full featured reading experience. In the meanwhile you can browse the magazine while it is downloading.

Reading text

For select articles and magazines we've extracted the text so it is more legible and resizable. To see the text view for these magazines, tap on the text icon button in the left side of the bottom of the screen. Click on the close icon to return to page layout.

Landscape viewing

You can see two pages of a spread at a time at full screen by rotating your Android device into landscape orientation. Rotate your device back for portrait viewing.

Table of contents

The icons at the bottom right of the screen open the table of contents, in a visual grid mode or an article list mode. For magazines that have text articles the Text Table of Contents will give you a list of text articles. Click on any article to jump to the text version of the page. From there, you can see the graphic version of the page by using your Android device back button. You can close the table of contents without jumping to another page by using your Android device back button.

Jump to a page

Tap on the "grid view" button on the right of the navigation bar at the bottom of the screen - you can quickly scroll up and down. Tap on a page to view it at full page.

Why can't I read the text version of this page?

It's either because the magazine doesn't have any text article or because it's text from ads and some editorial pages which we don't create. The text button is only displayed on pages that have a text version and on the first page of the article. Navigate to the first page of the article to see the text button.

Buying Publications

How do I buy a magazine from my Android device?

If you are reading a magazine, select MENU and then Shop. From the Library you can select the Shop button on the lower toolbar. Select categories in Shop to see a full list of titles available for Android. Click on a title to purchase the current issue or an annual subscription using your credit card.

How do I buy from Zinio.com?

On your browser, go to www.zinio.com and login. You can choose from our full library of thousands of titles. However, not all titles are available on Android.

Your Zinio Account

How do I sign out?

Tap on Menu and select "More". Then select the "sign out" option. Tapping the "sign out" button on that screen will confirm before signing you out.

I forgot my password?

On the sign in screen, there is a button on the bottom left of the page that loads the "Forgot Password" page. Alternately, navigate to www.zinio.com in your web browser and click on the log in link, then click on the "Forgot Password" link on that page. Enter your email in the appropriate field and click submit. We'll send you a new password to your email address.

Can I use multiple accounts on my Android device?

No. If you want to access a second account, sign out then re-sign in with that email and password and tap "sign in".

How do I change my password?

Tap on MENU then "More". Then tap the "Change Password" option. Fill out your old and new passwords respectively.

About Zinio
